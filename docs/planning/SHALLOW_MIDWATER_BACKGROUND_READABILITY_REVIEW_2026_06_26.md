# Shallow/Midwater Background Readability Review - 2026-06-26

Status: review artifact for issue #294.

## Decision

Keep the shallow/midwater background study wired at its current subdued treatment.

Do not brighten, animate, parallax, or expand it until the current vertical-slice readability gate has another normal-scale play pass. The study is helping the ocean feel less flat, but it should remain environmental atmosphere rather than a player-facing cue.

## Readability Checks

Resources:

- Pass with watchlist. The background uses muted teal and blue-green silhouettes, avoiding the strong yellow, green, shell, and glow reads used by collectible resources.
- Future resource reviews should confirm `Glow Plankton` still wins in the deep-reward route and that `Shell Fragments` remain readable near Shell Reef.

Scan targets:

- Pass. The study is not in the scan-target hierarchy, has no scan marker, and does not use bracket, diamond, pulse, or hot-cyan signal language.
- It should not gain local highlights that resemble `ScanPulseVisual`, Wreck Echo pulse, or selected-target tinting.

Safe return:

- Pass with watchlist. The study sits behind return-column/rib/beacon helpers and avoids the brighter safe-return cyan/green lane language.
- If first-run players miss the base direction, adjust return cues or HUD copy first rather than making the background more directional.

Pressure gate and cache:

- Pass. The study avoids pressure-gate bar geometry, lock-badge shapes, cache-core brightness, and orange/amber gate language.
- The pressure wreck/cache stack should remain the only strong blocked-promise/mystery read in the lower route.

Predator lane:

- Pass. The study avoids red, warning-current edges, tooth/jaw silhouettes, and predator marker contrast.
- Do not add darker foreground shapes near the Gulper route until predator-lane screenshots confirm the danger read still wins.

HUD contrast:

- Pass. The study is world-space and low-alpha; the compact active HUD and surface panels remain higher contrast.
- Any future foreground/background art must continue to respect HUD panel legibility before normal-scale polish is accepted.
