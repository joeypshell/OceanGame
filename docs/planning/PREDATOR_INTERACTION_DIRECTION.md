# Predator Interaction Direction

Predators should create route pressure before they become content to defeat. The first fantasy is ecological research under danger: observe, understand, avoid, and eventually prepare tools that let the player cross contested routes.

## Long-Term Progression

1. Observe: notice patrols, warning currents, silhouettes, and behavior tells.
2. Avoid: choose safer routes, wait for openings, or abandon a deep reward.
3. Distract or repel: use a non-lethal tool to recover from mistakes or cross a risky gap.
4. Tag or trap: mark a predator or temporarily control a route through preparation.
5. Selectively hunt or harvest: only if the game later commits to dangerous-specimen contracts or ecological harvesting as a central fantasy.

## Current Direction

The near-term game should lean toward ecological research and dangerous route management, not combat. Predator interactions should support decisions about oxygen, cargo, scans, and return timing.

## Deferred Until Validated

- Weapons.
- Health bars or damage races.
- Multiple predator types for content volume alone.
- Hunting contracts before non-lethal counterplay works.
- Harvesting predators before the game's ethical and mechanical fantasy is clearer.
