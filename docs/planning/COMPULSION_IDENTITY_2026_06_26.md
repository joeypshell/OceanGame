# Compulsion Identity Direction - 2026-06-26

## Purpose

This note updates planning after a design evaluation of OceanGame's current prototype loop and market-facing identity.

The prototype is structurally on track, but the roadmap should aim more deliberately at the compulsion layer: the reason a player thinks "one more expedition day" after a dive ends.

## North Star

Every morning the alien ocean changes. Dive, scan, and survive long enough to bring back the knowledge that lets you go deeper tomorrow.

## Core Fantasy

The player is a researcher-pilot slowly learning a dangerous alien ocean that changes each expedition day.

The strongest identity is not "2D Subnautica roguelite" by itself. It is:

- alien ocean research,
- short daily expeditions,
- persistent geography,
- variable opportunity,
- knowledge that survives failure,
- upgrades that open forbidden routes,
- creatures with learnable behaviors,
- strange mysteries below the pressure line.

## What Changes In Planning

### Keep The Visual Clarity Foundation

The current visual-clarity milestone remains correct. Readability is not cosmetic; it is the precondition for curiosity, fear, route choice, and world memory.

Visual clarity work should now serve the core fantasy explicitly:

- the player should recognize stable places,
- daily changes should be visible,
- forbidden routes should create desire before upgrades unlock them,
- danger should be readable before commitment,
- safe return should remain emotionally reassuring.

### Make The Day Loop Central

The in-game expedition day should become a central structure:

1. Morning: ocean condition report and current opportunity.
2. Dive: scan, gather, observe, avoid, and decide whether to push deeper.
3. Return or failure: cargo, scans, and lessons are resolved.
4. Evening: lab analysis and upgrade readiness create the next objective.
5. Sleep: the ocean shifts and the next seed/condition becomes active.

This should not become a restaurant, colony, farming, or base-management sim. The surface phase is a relief, analysis, upgrade, and "tomorrow changed" loop.

### Treat Regions As Memory Anchors

Depth bands are useful, but the player needs named places.

Each major region should eventually have:

- a fixed landmark,
- a variable daily opportunity,
- one major secret,
- one progression gate or promise,
- one creature behavior theme.

Initial region candidates:

- Surface Reef,
- Wreck Shelf,
- Thermal Vent Field,
- Pressure Trench,
- Abyssal Ruins.

The prototype should continue proving this with the current slice before adding broad region selection.

### Add A Mystery Spine

The game needs a lightweight mystery reason to care about "deeper."

Open questions can include:

- Why does the ocean shift overnight?
- Why do thermal blooms expose different routes?
- Why are predators guarding wreck corridors?
- What is below the pressure trench?
- Who built the submerged structures?
- Why do some scans return impossible biological readings?

This does not require a large narrative system yet. It requires discoveries, scan phrasing, and upgrade promises that imply a coherent hidden world.

### Upgrade Access Over Stat Inflation

Future upgrades should unlock verbs, routes, or knowledge rather than only improve numbers.

Preferred upgrade roles:

- `Pressure Seal I`: enter pressure pockets and reach visible locked promises.
- `Burst Thruster`: create skilled clutch movement and oxygen tradeoffs.
- scanner upgrades: reveal enough to plan without becoming a minimap or checklist.
- predator tools: convert observation into safer route timing, not combat.
- cargo upgrades: create different extraction choices, not inventory management.

## Near-Term Roadmap Impact

Do not replace the active visual-clarity queue. Adjust its intent:

- #144 visual language should include alien-ocean research identity, daily-change readability, forbidden-route promise language, and mystery-supporting silhouettes.
- #157 monster research planning should focus on learnable creature behavior and research payoff, not hunting/combat.
- #158 scanner progression should support curiosity, partial information, and planning rather than exact checklist directions.
- #162 backlog replenishment should consider the day/sleep/ocean-change loop, first region identity map, and mystery spine before adding another large system.

## Future Issue Candidates

After the visual clarity foundation, prefer these before broad new content:

1. Update implemented surface flow into a simple morning/evening/sleep rhythm.
2. Plan the first region identity map: Surface Reef, Wreck Shelf, Thermal Vent Field, Pressure Trench.
3. Plan the first mystery spine discoveries and scan tone.
4. Prototype a morning ocean-condition report that gives partial planning information.
5. Prototype evening lab analysis that makes failures and scans feel productive.

## Guardrails

Do not use this direction to add:

- a restaurant,
- farming,
- relationship systems,
- broad base management,
- full procedural biomes,
- real-calendar daily challenges,
- weapons,
- huge crafting trees,
- a minimap or full field guide.

The direction is stronger compulsion through expedition rhythm, world mystery, and access progression, not larger scope for its own sake.
