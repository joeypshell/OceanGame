# Area 02 Glass Rim Expansion

## Why This Exists

The first Outer Shelf pass created a remembered place, a survey payoff, a quiet `RIM SEAL NEEDED` promise, and deterministic evidence. The evidence also showed the area can still read too sparse and dark in normal framing. The next work should make Area 02 feel like a larger playable place without returning to broad polish or architecture-only churn.

## Milestone Goal

Turn Outer Shelf / Glass Rim into a small expedition arc:

`I reached the wider shelf, learned how to read the Glass Rim timing, chose between cargo and knowledge, and know what I want to try tomorrow.`

## Player-Visible Requirements

- Outer Shelf should read as a bigger side-view area at normal camera scale, with clearer terrain bands and local landmarks.
- Glass Rim should create one timing or current-reading decision that changes how the player moves or chooses oxygen risk.
- Glass Ray behavior should teach the route as a moving clue, not as combat, loot, capture, or a field-guide objective.
- The player should face one cargo-vs-knowledge or safety-vs-depth choice beyond the current survey.
- Extraction results should remember the choice and tease the next run without exact locator/checklist/map behavior.

## Guardrails

- No minimap, route graph, quest checklist, exact scanner locator, coordinate readout, or broad objective UI.
- No combat loop, harvesting, creature loot, creature inventory, or broad field-guide system.
- No full biome overhaul, final art pass, management layer, or broad crafting tree.
- No new upgrade unless it points back to a visible remembered place.
- Keep visual tests targeted to route/camera changes; use quick logic tests for state and guardrails.

## Ordered Work Batch

1. Strengthen Outer Shelf terrain/readability so the first Area 02 route reads as a place rather than empty dark water.
2. Add one Glass Rim timing/current decision that can be read in motion without changing global oxygen tuning.
3. Make Glass Ray Drifter behavior teach the Glass Rim timing read.
4. Add one beyond-rim cargo-vs-knowledge payoff choice.
5. Add extraction/result memory that reflects the Glass Rim choice and teases one next-run hypothesis.
6. Refresh deterministic evidence only after the visible route changes exist.

## Stop Conditions

Pause and recalibrate if the queue drifts back into architecture-only work, screenshot-only work, local UI polish, exact locator behavior, or route breadth without a normal-play reason to dive again.
